Self-Quarantine

How it works

Self-quarantine involves staying at home and avoiding contact with others when you are exposed to or infected with a contagious disease. This helps prevent the spread of the disease to others. Self-quarantine typically lasts for the incubation period of the disease, which can vary depending on the illness. During this time, individuals should monitor their health for symptoms and seek medical advice if needed.

Steps you can take

Individuals can:

  • Stay home and avoid going out unless absolutely necessary.
  • Separate from others, use a separate room and bathroom if possible.
  • Monitor health by keeping track of any symptoms and seek medical attention if they worsen.
Organizations can:
  • Support employee by providing remote work options and flexible leave policies.
  • Clearly outline quarantine protocols and support services available.

Key times for prevention

Key times for prevention All of the prevention strategies described in this guidance can be helpful to reduce risk. They are especially helpful when:

  • After exposure to a contagious disease.
  • When experiencing symptoms of a contagious illness.
  • During widespread outbreaks or pandemics.
