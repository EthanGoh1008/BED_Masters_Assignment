Medical Testing

How it works

Medical testing involves conducting diagnostic tests to detect the presence of diseases or infections. Early detection through testing can help manage and control the spread of infectious diseases by identifying and isolating affected individuals. Common types of medical testing include PCR tests, antigen tests, and antibody tests, each serving different purposes in detecting current or past infections.

Steps you can take

Individuals can:

  • Get tested, if experiencing symptoms or after exposure to a contagious disease.
  • Follow guidelines, adhere to testing recommendations from health authorities.
  • Report results, share test results with healthcare providers for proper follow-up and guidance
Organizations can:
  • Facilitate access to testing, provide or coordinate testing services for employees and the community.
  • Educate the public about testing, inform people about the importance and availability of testing.

Key times for prevention

Key times for prevention All of the prevention strategies described in this guidance can be helpful to reduce risk. They are especially helpful when:

  • Before attending large gatherings or traveling to areas with high infection rates.
  • When experiencing symptoms of a contagious illness.
  • After potential exposure to a contagious disease.
