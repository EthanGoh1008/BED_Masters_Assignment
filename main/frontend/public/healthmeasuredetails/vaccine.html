Vaccination

How it works

Vaccination involves administering a vaccine to stimulate the body's immune system to recognize and fight specific pathogens, such as viruses or bacteria. Vaccines contain weakened or inactive parts of a particular organism (antigen) that triggers an immune response within the body. This immune response helps the body recognize and fight the pathogen in the future, thereby providing immunity against the disease. Vaccines are highly effective in preventing the spread of infectious diseases and have been instrumental in controlling outbreaks and eradicating diseases like smallpox.

Steps you can take

Individuals can:

  • Ensure you are up to date with all recommended vaccines.
  • Keep track of vaccination schedules and any booster doses that may be needed.
  • Discuss any concerns or questions about vaccines with healthcare professionals.
Organizations can:
  • Provide easy access to vaccination services for employees and the community.
  • Distribute accurate information about the benefits and safety of vaccines.

Key times for prevention

Key times for prevention All of the prevention strategies described in this guidance can be helpful to reduce risk. They are especially helpful when:

  • During outbreaks of infectious diseases.
  • Before traveling to areas with high infection rates.
  • For vulnerable populations such as the elderly, young children, and individuals with compromised immune systems.
