Fresh Air Exposure

How it works

Ensuring good ventilation and access to fresh air can reduce the concentration of airborne pathogens indoors. Proper ventilation helps dilute and remove airborne contaminants, including viruses and bacteria, reducing the risk of infection. Opening windows, using air purifiers, and ensuring HVAC systems are well-maintained can improve air quality indoors.

Steps you can take

Individuals can:

  • Open windows to allow fresh air to circulate indoors whenever possible.
  • Use air purifiers, consider using HEPA filters to clean indoor air.
  • Spend time outdoors when safe and feasible, spend time in well-ventilated outdoor spaces.
Organizations can:
  • Improve ventilation systems by ensuring HVAC systems are functioning properly and filters are regularly replaced.
  • Promote outdoor activities, encourage activities in open, outdoor spaces where the risk of airborne transmission is lower.

Key times for prevention

Key times for prevention All of the prevention strategies described in this guidance can be helpful to reduce risk. They are especially helpful when:

  • After exposure to a contagious disease.
  • In crowded indoor environments.
  • When indoor air quality is poor due to pollution or other factors.
