Keeping Clean

How it works

Maintaining cleanliness through regular handwashing, sanitizing surfaces, and personal hygiene practices helps reduce the transmission of germs and pathogens. Proper hand hygiene involves washing hands with soap and water for at least 20 seconds or using hand sanitizer with at least 60% alcohol when soap and water are not available. Regular cleaning and disinfecting of frequently touched surfaces, such as doorknobs, light switches, and mobile devices, can prevent the spread of viruses and bacteria.

Steps you can take

Individuals can:

  • Wash hands regularly. Especially before eating, after using the restroom, and after coughing or sneezing.
  • Use hand sanitizer, when soap and water are not available.
  • Maintain personal hygiene. Regularly bathe, change clothes, and avoid touching the face with unwashed hands.
Organizations can:
  • Provide hygiene supplies. Ensuring the availability of soap, hand sanitizer, and cleaning supplies.
  • Promote cleanliness by encouraging regular handwashing and cleaning practices among staff and visitors.

Key times for prevention

Key times for prevention All of the prevention strategies described in this guidance can be helpful to reduce risk. They are especially helpful when:

  • During flu season or outbreaks of contagious illnesses.
  • After contact with potentially contaminated surfaces or individuals.
  • In high-traffic areas and public spaces.
